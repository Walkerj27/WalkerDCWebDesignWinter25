My Favorite Hobby is Fishing

I fished for the first time when I was eight with my dad, my grandpa, and one of my grandpa's friends. We went out early in the morning and caught a lot of fish which was way more than we had expected. After this outing, we had started to do this every year. Eventually, I started fishing in the ponds nearby with my friends and we would ride are bikes their and stay for hours. After this we never stopped and would go very often in the summer.

How I got Started

Usually when I go fishing, I will fish for largemouth bass, catfish, or carp. For ponds, these three species are quite common and they are the most fun to fish for. They can be found in many different parts of Michigan and other states too. There are many other kinds of fish in the Great Lakes like musky, sturgeon, lake trout, and many other kinds of bass. These are the best fish to go for as they usually will be the most fun to catch and the biggest.

Fish Species

Like stated earlier, largemouth bass, carp, and catfish will be the most common fish to try and catch. Along with these three, bluegill can be common too for new fishermen to help them learn to fish. Firstly, largemouth bass will ahve the most variety. They will bite a lot of different rigs, but it just depends on the conditions for that day. For example, if the water is muddy and it is a windy day then white lures will be the best as the will shine in the murky water. Carp love to bite corn or bread and they are the most active on the bottom of the pond during hot, sunny days. Lastly, catfish primarily bite shad or bluegill and these will usually be frozen before use.

Certain Baits for Certain Fish

  1. Soft Plastic Baits
      Soft plastics include senkos, bugs, and lizards. They are so versatile on a bass hook because there are so many ways you can rig them. A wacky rig and texas rig are usually the most commonly used, but there are many other that work just as well.
  2. Frogs
      Frogs are the best topwater lures out there. They are versatile and there are a few different ways to throw them out.
  3. Spinnerbaits
      Spinnerbaits are always a great go-to as they can be used in a lot of different situations and are quite reliable.
  4. Crankbaits
      There are lipped crankbaits and others with no lip. They both are very good, but the lipped crankbaits are the better choice for the most part. They can be fished at many different depths too.
  5. Jigs
      There are so many types of jigs that work in certain scenarios that they had to make the list. There is always a jig for any situation that will work well.
