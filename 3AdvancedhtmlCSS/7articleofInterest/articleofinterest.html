A College Basketball Season

The Regular Season

The regular season is the first section of the college basketball season. Most teams will have their first game at the start of November. The regular season is a period where each team will play about thirty games to decide their rankings for the bracket and the conference tournament, but can also a period where developing teams have time to build chemistry and figure out their style of play.

Conference Tournaments

Conference tournaments are technically part of the regular season, but they have such a large impact that they deserve a separate section. Conference tournaments include all of the teams in one conference to battle for an automatic bid into the NCAA Tournament. The seeding is based on the conference record of each team throughtout their conference play this season. The top four teams get two byes and the next four teams get a single bye. The conference tournament is so important for many teams because it gives them a chance at the tournament even if they played badly this year.

NCAA Tournament

The final part of the season is the NCAA Tournament where 64 teams are competing for a national championship. The tournament includes 6 rounds of a bracket where each team will face an opponent from their region before moving on. There are four regions in the bracket. The four regions are South, Midwest, East, and West. Each region has 16 teams that will battle for one spot in the final four. The winner of the entire bracket is then crowned the national champions.
