Is Stephen Curry a Top 3 Player of All Time

Many opinions vary, but the top 10 we are using is:

  1. Lebron James
  2. Michael Jordan
  3. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
  4. Shaquille O'Neal
  5. Tim Duncan
  6. Magic Johnson
  7. Kobe Bryant
  8. Steph Curry
  9. Kevin Durant
  10. Larry Bird

Where Steph is ranked at #8. But if you look deeper, you will see he could be a lot higher.

The main areas to look at are: Team Success and Individual Player Stats


At #7 is Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant and Steph Curry are both offensively strong guards that can score the ball at all 3 levels. While Kobe is a great player, stats show that Curry is a clear cut favorite above Kobe. While Kobe has one more championship than Curry, he won three of them with Shaquille O'Neal who is the fourth best player on our list. Curry also won two of his with Kevin Durant showing that both players won championships with great players and 2 without a solid team. Next, Steph beats Kobe in offensive scoring. They average about the same points per game, but Curry has a lot better efficiency shown through points per minute, field goal percentage, offensive ratings, and having a lower free throws per game. Lastly, Curry has more MVPs than kobe which rounds out his better individual and team success.

At #6 is Magic Johnson

Magic Johnson and Steph Curry are considered the two best point guards of all time. Steph Curry is cut as the superior scorer on all levels while Magic is one of the best playmakers the league has ever seen. Starting off with champshionships, Magic has won one more than Curry, but he never won a chip without Kareem Abdul-Jabbar who is the third best player on this list. Magic also was never the primary scorer on his team like Curry was every year for the Warriors. Offensively, Curry is a much better scorer while Magic is a better playmaker, but Steph is the best 3 point shooter ever shattering records that will stand for a long time. Magic, while a great playmaker, cannot compete with this level of offensive dominance. Lastly, Curry has revolutionized the game with his 3 point shooting which will change the game forever due to his impact.

At #5 is Tim Duncan:

Tim Duncan is a defensive forward while Steph is an offensive guard, so it is not too easy to compare their stats. Starting off, Steph averages 0.73 points per minute which is the 4th highest of all time meaning he is the fourth most efficient scorer as this stat measures efficiency. Steph also does this while averaging 4.4 free throws per game compared to 7.7 for Duncan which is about 2-3 less points per game. Steph also has double the assists per game that Duncan has showing he contributes on multiple different parts of the offense. Tim Duncan still beats Steph on most defensive and rebounding stats as that is the main part of his game. Although Duncan has great defense, he has never won Defense Player of the Year which is the best defensive accolade while Steph has won two scoring titles throughout his career for his offensive abilities. These two may be hard to compare with their different physicals and skillsets, but looking at the stats shows Steph was better at his role than Duncan was at his.
