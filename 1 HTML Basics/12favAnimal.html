Polar Bears

Introduction

Since I was young, my favorite animal to see at the zoo was the polar bears. A lot of the time they were sleeping because we would go in the summers when it was hot outside. Sometimes, though, the polar bears would be swimming around or fighting each other and overall being very active. These days were always the best and it made them the most fun to watch. Marine Mammal Center


Location and Habitat

Polar bears are primarily located in the arctic area in and near the countries of Canada, Russia, Norway, and more. They also are on the ocean's ice for most of time as they do many things there including hunting and resting. The habitat these mammals have in slowly diminishing though as climate change in melting the ice resulting in a smaller area for them to live. Marine Mammal Center


Hunting for Food

Typically, polar bears will hunt for ringed and bearded seals along with fish at other times. Polar bears require the fat from the seals for their body to to help keep them warm in their cold habitat. Also polar bears are fully dependent on the sea ice in the Arctic for hunting. Seals create small holes in the ice to dive below to search for their food. Polar bears use these holes to find seals and will hunt for the seals once they rise above the ice. Polar bears will also hunt for seals when the seals are sleeping and will pounce once they have an opportunity. The best time for the bears to hunt is often the springtime.
